The book contains details about the weave, and its leylines. The book begins with the old story of the old gods. Once, there was a god in an infinite realm, with nothing in it. Those pieces themselves were filled with such turbulence that they could not maintain their shape, so each split again into two, totalling six primordial gods. Death and Life, Chaos and Order, Magik and Hunger. Each of these gods made things. Life made the Living Realm, where all things breathe and are born. Death made the Next Place. Together, they made the Grey, also known as the Threshold, the place of transition between their realms. Life also made the Living, but Hunger, as it does, made Desire and the need to consume, so Death was pulled in, made to take away the beautiful creations of Life when they lost to Hunger. Chaos saw these living things and gave them Choice. Order saw how change occurred and made Time. Magik gave the living creatures Hope, Miracles, and Dreams. But Hunger, seeing all that was given, wanted to take something away, to Consume something.

On the next page there is information about Leylines, Keylines, and Crossroads. Leylines are threads of the weave that for one reason or another, possess more Mystical energy than others. The place where two or more Leylines cross is called a Crossroad. These sites tend to attract those who draw on Mystical energy. A Crossroad can also form at a site where a large amount of Mystical energy was interacted with or expressed. For exam, if a large amount of witches die in a location, that location becomes a Crossroad. Veils tend to be fairly thin at these locations. Beware that they could use these locata to cross into our realm. This cannot be allowed to happen. There are also some beliefs that Warlocks gain their power at Crossroads. Keylines are threads of the Weave that eminate from a Crossroad. They tend to lead Mystically inclined people to Crossroads whether or not they're aware of it. Beware of the Crossroads.
